TYPES OF TRUCKING PERMIT

  • Oregon permits
  • Motor carrier permits

OREGON PERMITS

Oregon is one of the states with permission to charge road tax. They issue Aspire DOT>Oregon Permit to commercial drivers operating within and through the state. Also, the tax rate depends on the weight of the vehicles. To register for an Oregon permit, you must provide some documents. The documents include USDOT Number, VIN, Vehicle Information, date of travel, driver full legal name, route, and others.

The easiest way to obtain your Oregon permits is through Global Multi services. You won’t have to wait for a lengthy application or profuse paperwork. We will file it stress-free and error-free.

MOTOR CARRIER PERMITS

Motor Carrier permit indicates the registration of your carrier identification number with the Department of Motor Vehicles. It also proves you met all the requirements to operate commercially across the United States. The permit is given by the Department of Motor Vehicles of the Motor Carrier Services. It also contains information like Name, Mailing address, permit, and expiration date. Let Global Multi-Services take care of the information and documents required by the Department of Motor Vehicles to register your trucking company. We are what you need, and we got you covered.

UCR PERMITS

Trucking companies involved in interstate commerce must obtain UCR permits and pay an annual fee to travel interstate and internationally. These fees are paid according to the size of their fleet and renewed yearly. To register, they must have USDOT and MC Numbers. Failure to obtain adherence can lead to truck detention by the officials and penalty fees depending on your base state.

UCR Registration is done online through their official website in your base state. A registration fee is charged after completing the registration. Trucking companies whose base state does not participate in the UCR program can apply for UCR Permit in their neighboring participating states.

NEW MEXICO PERMITS 

Trucking companies in New Mexico must pay taxes at the end of each quarter and register for New Mexico Permit. These taxes are charged based on the vehicle’s weight and distance traveled. The state also provides temporary permits for trucking companies traveling across the state for commercial purposes. It is obtained at the port of entry and is valid for 72 hours.

KENTUCKY PERMITS

Commercial vehicles operating interstate or intrastate with a gross weight of 60,000 pounds must register, obtain Kentucky Permits and pay tax quarterly. The tax is paid according to miles travel and mileage tax rate. Trucking companies traveling across the Kentucky highway must also obtain a temporary permit to avoid penalties. Trucking companies in the United States traveling across Canada must register and obtain a Canada Permit to operate legally.
