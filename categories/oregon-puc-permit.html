Oregon Permit

When it comes to the trucking industry, safety and permits such as Oregon Permit are paramount. According to research, the industry has one of the highest numbers of accidents and fatalities every year in the United States.  The truck drivers long working hours often make them lethargic while driving, which is considered unsafe. To improve safety and compliance, truck drivers need to follow strict regulations on the road. One mistake can cause severe damage to the driver, other road users, and truck. But these can be averted by adhering religiously to safety regulations, making the road a better and safer place for the drivers and other people on the road. Read on as Global Multi Services takes us through how truck drivers Keep their Compliance, Safety, Accountability Low.  

Pre-Trip and Post-Trip Inspection

 One of the best ways to keep your Compliance, Safety, and Accountability low is by performing pre-trip and post-trip inspections. Inspect every truck part to ensures it is in good condition. It is essential to use tools that document inspection accuracy. Drivers are the most vital asset in the trucking industry. Therefore, they must rest and be healthy before driving. According to the Department of Transportation, many truck accidents happen due to inclement weather. Hence drivers need to check the weather condition to stay safe. 

Avoid Distracted Driving 

 According to the Federal Motor Carrier Safety Administration, distraction while driving is one of the major causes of accidents for truck drivers. These occur when drivers take their eyes off the road or their hands off the steering wheel. Distractions can be caused by eating or chit-chatting, staring at a billboard or group of people, and many more. But the most common form of distracted driving is using the cellphone while driving. 

Obtain Oregon Permit and Other Documents

 Motor carriers must obtain all mandatory permits and license to stay in compliance. Each state also has laid down regulations and permits required to travel through them. Violating those regulations can lead to hefty fines and penalties. For example, the state of Oregon requires trucking companies to obtain their Oregon Truck Permit to operate commercially in Oregon. Obtaining the Oregon PUC Permit can sometimes be tedious that can cause delays in business. But with the help of Global Multi-Services, you will get your Oregon Permits within a short time and without stress. 

Avoid Safety Hazard

 Truck drivers must be proactive to safety hazards and their environment. These include looking out for sharp objects, animals, potholes, and other vehicles on the road. During bad weather, drivers can stop driving until the view is clear if they can’t see clearly. They should also ensure their windshield and lighting system are working perfectly. 

Have Proper Rest and Break 

 Truck drivers are mandatory to take break and rest when they feel sleepy or impaired while driving. These ensure they don’t doze off while driving and also help regain their strength. Trucking companies should encourage their drivers to prioritize their safety and the safety of others by taking out time, especially, at night to rest well.
