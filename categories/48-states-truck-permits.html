The trucking industry is prey to challenges, and the more informed you are about the specific vulnerabilities, the more prepared you will be. These challenges range far and wide, and no one knows when the dead-end will come. Many factors have also increased these challenges. They include advanced technology, driver shortages, and even international trade disputes.

The trucking industry issues are inevitable but have a lot to offer if you are ready and willing to focus on doing it right. Buckle up as Aspire DOT takes you through the trucking industry challenges in the United States.

  1. Driver shortage
  2. Constantly changing regulations
  3. Deteriorating infrastructure
  4. Financing a trucking company

DRIVER SHORTAGE

One of the biggest challenges the trucking industry experience in the United States is the shortage of truck drivers. According to reports, the trucking industry has more than 63,000 driver shortages. Also, only about 6% stay in their position every year. Reports from the American Trucking Association also indicate that it might grow to 100,000 by 2022.

CONSTANTLY CHANGING REGULATIONS

The trucking industry is subject to constant review and revision. Each state in the United States also has different regulations. These make it difficult to operate and stay compliant in the industry. Trucking companies must now install devices that track the hours their trucks spend on the road every day. The Federal Motor Carrier Safety Administration has also proposed lengthening shifts, changing rules regarding breaks, and others.

DETERIORATING INFRASTRUCTURE

Having a safe and reliable infrastructure is crucial for operating a successful trucking company. That is why trucking companies have an infrastructure spending plan. But these come with a cost. Asphalt deterioration, signage issues, construction delays, crumbling bridges, roads, and tunnels. All these affect the delivery of goods and cause accidents
